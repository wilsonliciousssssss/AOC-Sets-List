# Set/List — a DJ's gig playlists

*Alpha Omega Collective*

This is a little website for sharing the music behind a DJ set. Before a gig, the
set gets planned out here — every track, in the order it'll be played — and this
site puts it somewhere you can just open on your phone and look at.

Think of it as the **menu for the night**: what's coming, in what order, and how it
all flows from the first track to the last.

## What you'll find here

The **home page** is a list of gigs. Each one shows up as a coloured card with the
date, the venue, and a quick snapshot — how many tracks, the tempo range, and how
long the set runs. Upcoming gigs are at the top; once a date has passed it slides
down into "Past Sets" on its own.

Tap any card and you open that gig's **set sheet** — the full playlist, laid out
like a map of the night:

- **Every track in play order** — song, artist, and the moment it lands in the set.
- **The energy curve** — a line that rises and falls, so you can see the warm-up,
  the peaks, the breather, and the big finish at a glance.
- **Keys & BPM** — the musical key and tempo of each track. DJs use these to blend
  one song smoothly into the next so the music never jars. There's a colour "key
  wheel" that shows how it all fits together.
- **Phases** — the set is split into chapters (a gentle opening, a first build, a
  peak, and so on), each with its own colour, so the journey is easy to follow.

You don't need to know any of the technical stuff to enjoy it — the colours and the
rising-and-falling energy line tell the story on their own. The details are there
for anyone curious about *how* a set is built.

## How to use it

Nothing to install, nothing to sign up for. Open the link, scroll the gigs, tap one
to see its playlist. It works on your phone, tablet or computer, and you can share
the link with anyone. Tap the little coloured squares in the top corner to recolour
the page if you fancy a different look.

## The look

The whole site is built in the **Alpha Omega Collective** style — deep-ink
background, sharp edges, bold type, and a set of bright signature colours (the
"channels"). Each gig gets its own colour, so the list stays lively while everything
still feels like one family. It's the same design language across the whole thing:
*one system, many channels.*
